This visualization represents a jogger's running statistics. By using interactive and graphic representations, as well as numbers, it aim to give relevant and easy-to-understand informations about your runs. You can visualize the different itineraries you run, and compare different runs from the same itinerary, to follow your performance.

This project was made during the Data Vizualisation course of a Data Master Degree at Lyon 1.

The data comes from the application Zeopoxa Courir. You can find more details about our inspirations and other source on the GitHub page of the project..

On the top-left window, circles enable to show you your regularity when you run. Each circle represents an itinerary. You can define two speed thresholds, and the circles are going to divide into three colors : one for the time you spent under the first speed threshold, one for the time you spent between the first and the second threshold, and one for the time you spent over the second speed threshold. Each color area is proportional to the amount of time spent.

By clicking on an itinerary among the circles, you get informations about this itinerary on the top-right window. This provides general informations about the track. On the bottom-right window, you can visualize for each run the monitoring of your speed as a function of the distance. You can choose to show as many runs from the same track as you want, in order to compare them. You also have information about the evolutuion of altitude in blue. By clicking on a run, you get specific informations about it on the bottom-left window.
